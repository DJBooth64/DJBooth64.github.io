Research


The real estate market at the City of Boston demonstrates a diverse body of ownerships. Click each city to see what companies they have in Boston. Click the top white bar to go back.

What type of properties would you like to look at?

The above visualization allows the viewer to understand the most valuable properties by city designation in Boston. Red values correspond to commercial properties, green to residential, and blue to other (mostly industrial). We immediately find that the commercial properties are by far the most valuable, as the top 20 properties are all commercial properties. However, even the industrial properties (having the lowest maximum value) have values in the hundreds of millions of dollars, speaking to the size of industry in the city and the high value in this real estate market.

What type of properties would you like to look at?

The above visualization allows the viewer to sort by the same criteria as above, but view a histogram of all such properties by the number of floors in the building. All the histograms are significantly right-skewed, in that the vast majority of buildings have a small number of floors, but there certainly are a fair number of outliers at the top. Sorting by commercial allows the viewer to see the large number of skyscrapers in Boston, and both commercial and other properties have a large proportion of land for future development or conservation (meaning no building on the property). Finally, residential properties have the smallest right tail, as nearly all land between 1 and 6 floors.

Property Bundles

Use this visualization to explore the values of different property bundles. The buttons allow you to add random properties to the given bundle or remove the most recently added item.




Add or remove properties from these bundles, then compare!


Bundle 1: Red


Bundle 2: Blue

If a purple bar appears, that property is contained in both bundles.

This visualization models the process of investing in Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs), bundles of properties which are sold as a group instead of as individual pieces. By adding properties to each bundle, the bars in the central chart represent all the properties in each bundle and the bar chart on the right is a comparison of the total values of each bundle, with the higher valued bundle behind the smaller one. Although the addition and deletion from these bundles is random, the principles of the investment idea are conveyed through the exploration of different packages of properties.
